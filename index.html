3- The Story of a Club Like No Other

It all started in 1886, in south London, when a group of workers at a weapons factory decided to form a football team. They called it Dial Square, but soon changed the name to Arsenal. From day one, the club had a fighting spirit — built by workers, for the people. In the early days, we moved to Highbury, and that stadium became our home for over 90 years. Legends played there. Dreams were made there. Then came the golden years... Arsène Wenger arrived in 1996 and changed everything. He brought beautiful football, big trophies, and global respect. We went invincible in 2003–04 — 38 games, no losses. No one else has done it in Premier League history. That team? Pure magic. After Wenger, things got shaky — new managers, ups and downs. But the fans? We stayed loyal. Because Arsenal is family. Now under Mikel Arteta, the project is alive again. Young players, exciting football, and big dreams. We’re back fighting for the league, back in the Champions League, and back to being proud. From the factories of Woolwich to the world stage — Arsenal is more than a club. It’s a feeling. It’s us.

4- Arsenal Legends – Heroes of Our Story

Over the years, many great players wore the Arsenal shirt and became legends — not just for their skills, but for their hearts and love for the club. First, there was Dennis Bergkamp — the “Non-Flying Dutchman.” A genius on the ball, he made impossible passes and scored unforgettable goals. He brought class and creativity that changed our game forever. Then came Tony Adams, the captain, the rock. Born and raised in Arsenal’s youth, he led the team with courage and strength. His leadership helped us win trophies and build the Arsenal spirit. And who can forget Thierry Henry? The greatest Arsenal goalscorer of all time, a true legend. Fast, skillful, and deadly in front of goal. He gave us moments of pure magic and made us dream big. There’s also Patrick Vieira, the midfield warrior. Strong, fearless, and a true leader on the pitch. He battled in the middle and inspired the team to fight for every ball. These legends shaped who we are. Their names are written in our hearts — and their spirit lives on in every player who wears the Arsenal shirt today. From Bergkamp’s genius to Henry’s goals — these legends are the soul of Arsenal.
